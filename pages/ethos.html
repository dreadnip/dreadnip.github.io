Ethos

A good website is...

Small

Actively fight the web obesity crisis. Avoid large, complex systems. Question every link, include, script or image. If it doesn't actively contribute to the site's purpose or the user's experience, it shouldn't be there.

Keeping your site small and fast makes it accessible to more people.

Open source

Unless you have a very good reason not to, a site's source should be open source. Only by sharing our efforts, ideas and work can we progress to a more open internet.

Well documented

Code should be modifiable by someone that knows how to program but hasn't seen this particular codebase.

Accessible

Write semantic HTML. Follow the WCAG 2.0 guidelines. Use aria-attributes when necessary.

Guidelines

Unfinished

A website is a form of creative expression and allowed to be raw or unfinished. There's no need for every page of the internet to look like a Dribbble shot. The web operated for decades before these kinds of styles were even a thing. Focus on the content.
